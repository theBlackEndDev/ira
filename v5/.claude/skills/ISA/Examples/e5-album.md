## Problem

The artist has 18 months of accumulated sketches, voice memos, and half-finished arrangements scattered across a project folder, three notebooks, and an old field-recorder full of ocean and harbor sounds. None of it is a record. The work has stalled at the place every long-form creative project stalls: the gap between "lots of material" and "a thing the world can listen to from start to finish." The pattern across the artist's previous attempts has been the same — composition opens too many doors, mixing extends into tweaking forever, mastering gets handed to someone else and the result sounds like everyone else's record. This time the goal is to take 12 tracks from blank page to mastered release within a 6-month window without that pattern repeating.

## Vision

A 12-track instrumental album titled `Mariner Frequencies` — roughly 48 minutes of patient, harbor-adjacent ambient-electronic music threaded with field recordings made along the coast at dawn. Listeners encounter it on a streaming platform or a Bandcamp page, press play, and stop scrolling before track three. They listen all the way through, then go back to track 5 and listen to it three more times in a row. They text a friend a link with the words "play this with headphones." That is the euphoric surprise: the record holds attention for 48 minutes the first time and pulls the listener back to a specific track within the same listening session.

## Out of Scope

- **No vocals.** Instrumental record only. No lyric sheet, no vocalist features, no spoken word.
- **No live-band recording.** Solo project — synthesizers, programmed drums, processed guitars, field recordings. No drum kit miking, no string sections, no ensemble.
- **No physical media in v1.** Streaming + Bandcamp digital release first. Vinyl is a v2 conversation if the album lands.
- **No music videos.** A single static cover image and twelve track titles is the entire visual surface for release.
- **No tour.** This is a recorded-music project. Live performance is a separate decision after release.
- **No major-label submission.** Self-released through Bandcamp + DistroKid to streaming. No A&R outreach.
- **No collaborations on this record.** Outside engineers (mixing, mastering) are paid services, not creative co-authors. No "featuring" credits.

## Principles

- A finished mediocre record beats an unfinished masterpiece. The album exists to be heard, not to be perfect.
- Constraints are the friend of taste. A 6-month window with a fixed track count forces decisions; an open-ended schedule has historically produced no album at all.
- Field recordings are first-class compositional material, not decoration. The harbor at dawn is the second instrument on this record.
- Patience over density. The reference points are records that breathe. If a section feels like it needs another layer, it probably needs less.
- The mix is for headphones first, car speakers second, laptop speakers third. The record is being made for people who put on headphones intentionally.
- Mastering is a craft this artist does not have. It gets paid for and outsourced to someone whose ear is calibrated for streaming-platform delivery.

## Constraints

- 12 tracks. Total runtime between 42 and 54 minutes. Track 1 ≤ 4 minutes (entry); track 12 ≤ 6 minutes (exit).
- Release date: 2026-05-15 (180 days from the first composition session). The release is calendared, not aspirational.
- Recording / production budget cap: $1,800. This covers mastering ($600), one outboard preamp rental ($120), Bandcamp Pro annual ($120), DistroKid annual ($30), studio monitors calibration ($150), and reserve ($780).
- Production environment: home studio, Logic Pro, two synth modules, one electric guitar, one field recorder. No additional hardware purchases.
- Field recordings sourced from coastal locations within 90 minutes of the studio. Recordings must be made by the artist, not licensed.
- Mixing done by the artist; mastering done by a paid third-party engineer with delivery format LUFS −14 integrated for streaming.
- Reference album for sonic palette: a 2018 ambient-electronic record the artist has listened to ~200 times — used as A/B comparison during mix, not as imitation target.

## Goal

Produce, mix, master, and release a 12-track instrumental album titled `Mariner Frequencies` between 2025-11-01 and 2026-05-15, totaling 42–54 minutes, mixed by the artist and mastered by a paid third-party engineer to streaming-spec LUFS, distributed via Bandcamp and DistroKid, with at least one track that the artist would still defend as a representative work five years later.

- [ ] ISC-20: Stereo image is centered for sub-bass, narrowing to a clear center on percussive elements, with width preserved for ambience and reverb returns.
- [ ] ISC-21: Each mix bounced as 24-bit/48kHz WAV with ≥ 6dB of headroom below 0dBFS.

### Mastering (Phase 5: Mar–Apr 2026)

- [x] ISC-33: Anti: scope creep — total track count never exceeds 12 in the final sequence; "bonus tracks" are not added late.
- [ ] ISC-34: Anti: over-engineered mix — no track has more than 24 simultaneous audio tracks at the busiest section (probe: count of non-muted tracks per project at the densest bar).
- [ ] ISC-35: Anti: filler track 7 — track 7 (the structural midpoint) survives a "would I keep this if it cost me a week of life?" test by the artist on 2026-02-10 and is not the lowest-rated track in the listener test.
- [ ] ISC-36: Anti: runtime overrun — final sequence runtime stays ≤ 55:00; 56-minute albums get skipped in 2026's listening culture.
- [ ] ISC-37: Anti: mastered-too-quiet — no mastered track measures below −16 LUFS integrated (Spotify-loudness-target failure mode).
- [ ] ISC-38: Anti: license-tripwire — no software instrument used in the final mix requires an active subscription (probe: project loads on a fresh install of Logic with only first-party plugins + the two paid plugins owned outright).
- [ ] ISC-39: Anti: late-stage rewrite — no compositional edits to track structure after 2026-02-15 sequence-lock; mix work is mix work, not composition.
- [ ] ISC-40: Anti: cover-art bottleneck — cover art is finalized by 2026-04-10 (≥ 3 weeks before release) so it does not become the gating dependency.

## Decisions

- 2025-11-01 16:00: 12 tracks chosen over 9 or 15 — 9 felt like an EP, 15 was the inflated arc that has stalled prior projects. 12 is short enough to finish and long enough to feel like a record.
- 2025-11-08 14:00: Field-recording site shortlist locked: harbor at dawn (primary), tidepool reef at low tide, salt-marsh boardwalk, an empty pier at first light. Four locations within a 90-minute drive.
- 2025-11-22 10:00: Reference album chosen as A/B target, not imitation target. Reference will be muted on the final mix-bus comparison; the goal is "would this fit on the same shelf," not "does this sound the same."
- 2025-12-04 17:00: ❌ DEAD END: Tried the modular-synth rabbit hole — three weeks lost building patches that were never used in any sketch. Reverted to the two existing synth modules and committed to "no new instruments enter the project after Dec 15." Don't retry.
- 2025-12-30 22:00: refined: ISC-3 candidate count raised from 12 to 14 — the +2 backup gives optionality if a candidate fails to develop, without inflating the album scope.
- 2026-01-12 09:00: Mastering engineer selected from a shortlist of three based on a paid 30-second test pass on Track 5; chosen engineer's pass made Track 5 sound like the album it was supposed to be. Deposit due 2026-03-15.
- 2026-01-28 19:30: ❌ DEAD END: Tried mixing into a soft-clipping bus limiter to "save time" by approximating mastering during mix. Result: mixes were 4 LU louder than they should have been, killed the mastering engineer's headroom, and his first pass came back asking for ≥ 6dB more bus headroom. Reverted; mix bus is now compression + saturation + tilt EQ only, no limiter. Don't retry.
- 2026-02-09 14:00: refined: ISC-13 codified the "long-track cut" rule after track 7's first arrangement was 9:14 and felt 3 minutes too long; the 60-second cut rule is now applied to tracks 4 and 10 as well even though neither felt overlong on first pass.
- 2026-02-15 18:00: refined: ISC-39 added — sequence is now locked, and any composition-level edits after this date roll into the next project. Mix work is the only allowed surface from this point.
- 2026-03-22 11:00: Cover art commissioned with a working brief (no people, harbor-dawn palette, generous negative space, single visual element); first sketch approved 2026-04-08 and final delivered 2026-04-10 — within the ISC-40 deadline.
- 2026-04-04 16:30: refined: ISC-37 added after a streaming-platform research pass — a too-quiet master gets normalized up by Spotify and AAC re-encoding, which is fine, but a master at −18 LUFS sounds anemic next to neighbors on autoplay queues. The −16 LUFS floor is the audible-floor in the 2026 listening environment.

  learned: mixing is decision-making, mastering is decision-making, and combining them on the same buss collapses two distinct judgment passes into one rushed one; the headroom rule (ISC-21) exists precisely to prevent this
  criterion now: ISC-19 sharpened — mix bus is compression + saturation + tilt EQ, no limiter; ISC-21's ≥ 6dB headroom rule is now non-negotiable

- 2026-02-09 | conjectured: Long tracks (≥ 8 minutes) earn their length if the development is interesting
  refuted by: track 7's 9:14 first arrangement was technically interesting throughout but emotionally flat after minute 6 — the second listen, the third listen, both stalled at minute 6
  learned: emotional arc has a shorter half-life than compositional arc; if a track is emotionally complete by minute 6, the compositional development past that point is exposition, not climax
  criterion now: ISC-13 added — the three longest candidates each cut ≥ 60 seconds before mix; rule applied to tracks 4 and 10 prophylactically

- 2026-04-04 | conjectured: Mastering loudness is mostly a technical compliance question — hit −14 LUFS for streaming and stop worrying
  refuted by: a research pass on how Spotify, Apple Music, and Tidal handle non-conforming loudness in 2026 showed that under-loud masters get normalized up but lose perceived "weight" in autoplay-queue context

  criterion now: ISC-37 added — anti-criterion that no mastered track measures below −16 LUFS integrated
